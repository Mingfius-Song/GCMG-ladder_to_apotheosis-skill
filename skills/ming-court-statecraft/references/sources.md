# Sources and Copyright Boundary

## Purpose

This skill is not a text archive. It is an original structural distillation layer for Ming imperial statecraft and bureaucratic politics. It strengthens Sir Humphrey-style analysis without copying modern books or replacing the Humphrey axis.

The user-facing promise is: "full mechanism distillation where useful for lessons", not "full book reproduction".

## Modern Thematic Signals

These modern works shape the topic boundary and reading direction, but their text must not be copied into this repository:

| Work / book family | Use in this skill | Boundary |
|---|---|---|
| *Ming Dynasty popular histories*, including the *Ming Chao Na Xie Shi Er* family | Broad narrative signal: emperors, ministers, wars, court politics, imperial psychology, reform, factional struggle. | Do not reproduce text, chapter structure, scenes, jokes, or long summaries. |
| Ray Huang, *1587, A Year of No Significance* / *Wanli Shiwunian* | Structural signal: Wanli, Zhang Juzheng, Shen Shixing, Hai Rui, Qi Jiguang, Li Zhi, moralized politics, fiscal-institutional limits. | Do not copy prose or reconstruct the book. Use high-level concepts and public historical facts. |
| Biographies or studies of Zhang Juzheng, Wanli, late Ming politics, and Ming institutions | Background for reform, grand secretariat, fiscal constraint, court faction, and institutional reversal. | Prefer public-domain classics or properly licensed scholarly summaries when quoting; otherwise paraphrase at high level. |

## Public-Domain and Historical Anchors

Use public-domain or classical anchors for factual grounding where possible:

- 《明史》
- 《明实录》 / Ming Veritable Records where available through lawful sources
- 《张文忠公全集》 for Zhang Juzheng-related primary material where lawfully available
- Public-domain classical institutional terms and historical events

## Copyright Rules

1. Do not include full copyrighted book text, pirated ebooks, or extracted chapters.
2. Do not create chapter-by-chapter substitutes for copyrighted modern works.
3. Do not copy distinctive phrasing, jokes, narrative scenes, or long passages.
4. If the user provides a short excerpt, analyze that excerpt within normal copyright limits.
5. Use public facts, structural abstraction, and original synthesis.
6. Keep this repository MIT-only for original skill instructions; MIT does not grant rights to third-party books.

## Research Notes

Useful structural themes identified from public descriptions and historical summaries:

- *Wanli Fifteenth Year* is commonly described as using Wanli, Zhang Juzheng, Shen Shixing, Hai Rui, Qi Jiguang, and Li Zhi as representative cross-sections of late Ming institutional limits.
- Public descriptions of that book emphasize a state where moral language, ritual legitimacy, fiscal weakness, and bureaucratic inertia constrain effective governance.
- Public descriptions of Ming popular histories emphasize a broad narrative of Ming emperors, court politics, war, institutions, and imperial statecraft.
- GitHub contains some ebook-like or full-text uploads of modern copyrighted books. This repository must not import, transform, or paraphrase those materials into a replacement product.

These notes are high-level source tracking only. They are not a substitute for the books and should not be expanded into copyrighted summaries.
